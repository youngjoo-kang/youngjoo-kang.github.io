01. UTT: Korean Speech Annotation Portfolio

Judgment framework research across ten annotation cases in Korean speech data: how judgment forms in the absence of official guidelines, documented so the reasoning can be verified and reused.

Domain
Speech Data Annotation (Korean)
Deliverables
10 cases · Executive Summary · Open Questions Map
Method
Judgment-layer case analysis

Documents

  • Introduction: purpose, case-file structure, and what this portfolio does not cover
  • Executive Summary: cross-case meta-analysis of the recurring judgment mechanism and the trajectory of shifting criteria

Case index

10 annotation cases summarized below. Full documentation maintained separately.

Case 01Classifying the spoken function of ambiguous words ("또/뭐/아"), building independent criteria where guideline and dictionary coverage run out
Case 02Judging lexical fillers ("막/뭔가/아이") and forming the hierarchy between linguistic-category and model-training criteria
Case 03Non-standard pronunciation spellings ("쫌/왜냐면은"): correction target or preserved spoken form
Case 04Words in the same colloquial category ("니가/그쵸/헐/짱이다") routed to different processing paths
Case 05The same sound ("아-/음-/응-") as filled pause or non-verbal expression
Case 06A dictionary-listed word without interjection meaning ("엇") placed in the judgment hierarchy
Case 07The same sound ("에") as verbal affirmation or non-verbal vocalization, and the order of applying criteria
Case 08A neologism ("짬바") and an unlisted contraction ("갖고") judged together through different processing paths
Case 09Hangul or Latin rendering of loanwords and foreign proper nouns, and the discovery of a missing routing rule
Case 10A spelling where the norm allows two correct answers ("그렇네요/그러네요"), the first case left genuinely unresolved
